Top 10 Fashion Industry Polluters

Understanding which brands contribute most to environmental damage

Based on recent environmental impact assessments, these fashion corporations have the largest carbon footprints and contribute significantly to textile waste, water pollution, and labor exploitation. This list is compiled from data by environmental watchdog organizations and sustainability research institutes.

Last Updated: January 2025

1

Shein

Annual CO2 Emissions: 6.3 million tons

Ultra-fast fashion retailer producing over 6,000 new styles daily. Known for extremely low prices, poor labor conditions, and massive textile waste.

High Carbon Footprint Labor Exploitation Textile Waste
2

H&M Group

Annual CO2 Emissions: 3.5 million tons

Despite sustainability pledges, H&M remains one of the largest fast fashion producers with significant environmental impact from production and transportation.

Greenwashing Overproduction Water Pollution
3

Zara (Inditex)

Annual CO2 Emissions: 3.2 million tons

Fast fashion giant with rapid production cycles leading to massive waste. Produces 450 million garments annually.

Fast Production Chemical Use Waste Generation
4

Nike

Annual CO2 Emissions: 2.8 million tons

Major sportswear brand with extensive supply chain emissions and synthetic material usage contributing to microplastic pollution.

Synthetic Materials Supply Chain Microplastics
5

Adidas

Annual CO2 Emissions: 2.5 million tons

Similar to Nike, heavy reliance on synthetic materials and global manufacturing contributes to significant environmental impact.

Polyester Use Energy Intensive Transportation
6

Gap Inc.

Annual CO2 Emissions: 2.1 million tons

Parent company of Gap, Old Navy, and Banana Republic with large-scale production and distribution networks.

Mass Production Inventory Waste Emissions
7

Forever 21

Annual CO2 Emissions: 1.8 million tons

Budget fast fashion retailer with rapid trend turnover and low-quality garments designed for short lifespans.

Low Quality Trend Chasing Disposable Fashion
8

Primark

Annual CO2 Emissions: 1.6 million tons

Ultra-low-cost fashion retailer with minimal transparency about supply chain and environmental practices.

Lack of Transparency Volume Production Low Wages
9

Boohoo Group

Annual CO2 Emissions: 1.4 million tons

Online fast fashion retailer targeting young consumers with extremely low prices and rapid trend cycles.

Labor Issues Quality Concerns Returns Waste
10

Fashion Nova

Annual CO2 Emissions: 1.2 million tons

Social media-driven fast fashion brand with extremely rapid production and significant waste from returns and unsold inventory.

Influencer Marketing High Returns Trend Waste

What Can You Do?

Avoid these brands when possible and support sustainable alternatives. Every purchase is a vote for the kind of fashion industry you want to see.
